# 02 — Variance-aware clearance field

## Scope

Stage 02 fuses the two elevation maps produced by 01 into a per-cell clearance scalar `c(x, y) = z_ceil − z_floor` and a configuration-aware feasibility scalar `f(x, y, s) = c − h_base − s − ε(x, y)`, where the safety margin ε tightens with the per-cell variance of the underlying observations through a chance-constraint reformulation. The contribution closes G2: the per-cell variance that the dual-instance Kalman pipeline already produces is consumed as the input to an additive constraint-tightening at the ceiling layer, structurally identical to the additive tightening used in stochastic floor-traversability MPC but applied to the second elevation instance and at no additional perception cost. The output is exposed downstream as `f`, its gradient, and a C¹-smooth CasADi B-spline interpolant updated at the elevation-map rate; the interpolant is the controller-agnostic interface consumed by either the lead NMPC or any HMPC drop-in.

## Problem statement

The lit-review survey of overhead-clearance encodings (§6.1, §6.5) reveals a perception-side asymmetry. Per-cell height variance is a published interface of the floor elevation mapping pipeline: Fankhauser 2018 §III-B establishes the Kalman update `σ_h^{2+} = σ_h^{2-} σ_p² / (σ_h^{2-} + σ_p²)` with measurement variance `σ_p² = J_S Σ_S J_S^T + J_Φ Σ_ΦIS J_Φ^T` (sensor-noise plus sensor-rotation contributions; sensor-position uncertainty is excluded by the robot-centric frame choice), and Miki 2022 §II-C ports that update to the GPU (eqns 1–2) with an added constant time-variance increment for un-updated cells. What both frameworks publish in the `variance` layer of `grid_map_msgs/GridMap` is this per-cell Kalman height variance — driven by the measurement noise model and propagated through the recursive update — not the full per-cell spatial covariance Σ_P_i that Fankhauser 2018 §III-D constructs for the map-fusion step (which incorporates accumulated robot-motion drift through eqns 14, 20, 21). The variance available to a downstream consumer at the framework's runtime interface is therefore *sensor-model-driven*, and the distinction matters for what δ_cal in the present construction is being asked to absorb.

The floor-traversability literature has consumed this variance through two distinct mechanisms — CVaR cost shaping with a relaxable hard position-risk constraint (Fan et al. 2021, STEP, where each risk factor is modelled R ~ N(μ, σ²) and the closed-form CVaR enters the SQP cost) and Gaussian-process / conformal calibration of confidence intervals (Muenprasitivej 2025). The analogous construction at the ceiling layer is absent from reviewed work, which uniformly treats the overhead safety margin ε as a static design parameter chosen conservatively to absorb worst-case sensor noise. The construction adopted here replaces that static ε with `ε(x, y)` that tracks the actual confidence of the underlying ceiling observations, structurally a **deterministic chance-constraint reformulation** in the Hewing 2020 / Lorenzen 2016 stochastic-MPC sense — a hard constraint with additive tightening proportional to the per-cell standard deviation — rather than a CVaR-in-cost penalty. This is a perception-side reformulation, not an architectural one, and generalises to any of the controller families admitted by §7.7. The construction must satisfy three downstream requirements: smoothness sufficient for gradient-based consumption (C¹ minimum), latency low enough to refresh at the 10 Hz mapping rate, and exposure as a query interface that the controller can evaluate at arbitrary `(x, y, s)` along the prediction horizon without re-rasterising the field.

## Theory

The clearance field is the cell-wise difference between the two elevation layers:

$$
c(x, y) \;=\; z_{\text{ceil}}(x, y) \;-\; z_{\text{floor}}(x, y).
$$

Under the floor-ceiling observation-independence assumption inherited from 01 (plausible — separate sensor returns, different incidence angles — but unverified on the platform's stream; flagged in 01's open questions), the per-cell clearance variance composes additively:

$$
\sigma^2_c(x, y) \;=\; \sigma^2_{z_{\text{ceil}}}(x, y) \;+\; \sigma^2_{z_{\text{floor}}}(x, y).
$$

The terms being added are **not** raw LiDAR measurement variances. `sim_validation_01c` (ADR 0009) shows that the framework's published per-cell σ² is the steady state of a Kalman draw-down against a constant per-second inflation; in HILDA's operating regime (σ²_meas ≫ inflation rate × inter-observation time) it sits at the √-floor `map_v* ≈ √(σ²_meas · time_variance · update_variance_fps · Δt)`. The published σ² therefore scales as √α_d in the sensor noise parameter and √Δt in local observation cadence, not linearly in either. The additive composition above is correct in form — variance composes additively for independent channels regardless of the Kalman+inflation transformation each layer applies — but `σ²_c` carries both layers' √-compression. Calibration and downstream interpretation must reason about this; see [decisions/0009-variance-pipeline-alpha-d-diagnostic.md](decisions/0009-variance-pipeline-alpha-d-diagnostic.md) for the algebra and per-mechanism contribution accounting.

The feasibility scalar for a given platform configuration is

$$
f(x, y, s) \;=\; c(x, y) \;-\; h_{\text{base}} \;-\; s \;-\; \varepsilon(x, y),
$$

with `h_base` the platform's base height (configuration-independent) and `s = q_1 + q_2` the combined sledge extension; the feasibility region is `{(x, y, s) : f ≥ 0}`. The safety margin ε is variance-aware:

$$
\varepsilon(x, y) \;=\; \varepsilon_{\text{base}} \;+\; \delta_{\text{cal}} \;+\; \lambda \sqrt{\sigma^2_{z_{\text{ceil}}}(x, y) + \sigma^2_{z_{\text{floor}}}(x, y)}.
$$

The three contributions decompose by source. `ε_base` absorbs irreducible factors that do not vary with map state (drill assembly extent, platform vibration amplitude, controller tracking error). `δ_cal` is a calibration term fitted offline to absorb the gap between the published `σ²_c` and the residual distribution of `c_true − ĉ` on real data. The 01c diagnostic (ADR 0009) decomposes that gap into three structurally distinct contributions, replacing the earlier three-shortfall list:

1. **The √-compression of the measurement-variance signal itself.** The chance-constraint identity was constructed against σ² = σ²_meas (a pure LiDAR-noise variance); the framework's published σ² is the √-compressed Kalman+inflation state. At λ = 3 the effective margin λ √σ²_published is structurally below `λ · σ_meas` for any fixed cell, by a factor that depends on the cell's observation cadence and how saturated it is against the inflation rate. δ_cal restores the missing margin on average across the corpus, at the deployment values of `time_variance` and `update_variance_fps`. Those two framework parameters become required co-parameters of the calibration: if either is changed between calibration and deployment, the fitted δ_cal is invalidated and must be re-fit. Both are committed to the calibration manifest and to the runtime-versus-calibration config audit on startup.
2. **Within-cell geometric spread invisible to the framework's per-point noise model.** The per-point noise term `α_d · rz²` is a pure-distance model and does not see local ceiling curvature, beam-edge transitions, or planar-deviation residuals within a cell. This gap was named contingently in earlier drafts under "mean-vs-min bias" gated on the suppression-rule firing rate; the diagnostic makes it unconditional. Whether or not the suppression rule fires, the published variance does not encode within-cell geometric spread, and δ_cal absorbs it through the calibration residual. The dense/sparse corpus split below (provisionally specified for the mean-vs-min term, originally contingent on the 01 sweep) is retained as load-bearing: the dense passes characterise the rate-and-floor-driven √-compression in isolation, the sparse passes add the geometric spread on top.
3. **The unobserved time-inflation contribution.** The `time_variance · update_variance_fps · Δt` term in the floor expression introduces an α_d-independent additive component to σ²_published that scales with local observation cadence (which depends on robot motion and FOV coverage in deployment, not on sensor properties). δ_cal cannot calibrate this away as a global constant — the term varies cell-to-cell with how recently each was observed. The calibration objective is therefore reformulated **on the residual distribution conditional on observation freshness**; see §Calibration protocol for the binning step.

The previous list folded model mis-specification, localisation drift (Fankhauser 2018 §III-B excludes sensor position uncertainty from the measurement update by construction; the drift-aware spatial covariance Σ_P_i lives in the map-fusion step §III-D that the `elevation_mapping_cupy` runtime interface does not expose), and the contingent mean-vs-min bias into three categories. The new decomposition supersedes it: model mis-specification and within-cell geometric spread are merged into (2); the drift contribution rolls into the residual distribution that δ_cal fits over, no longer carrying its own line because the √-compression of σ²_published dominates the gap on the dense overhead surfaces calibration targets; the mean-vs-min bias is no longer contingent on the suppression-rule firing rate per 01b's flat-threshold result.

λ sets the one-sided confidence level on the dangerous tail (true clearance below the estimated clearance). The chance constraint `Pr(c_true ≥ h_base + s + ε_base) ≥ 1 − α` with `α = Φ(−λ)` is reformulated deterministically by replacing the right-hand side with `1 − Φ(−λ) = Φ(λ)`: at λ = 3, Φ(3) ≈ 99.865 % coverage on the one-sided lower tail. (The familiar 99.7 % figure is the two-sided P(|Z|<3) = 2Φ(3)−1 from the empirical 68-95-99.7 rule; it is **not** the coverage of a one-sided margin at λ = 3 and should not be quoted here.)

**Calibration class.** The margin's functional form `λ √σ²_c + δ_cal` is shaped like the additive constraint-tightening of stochastic MPC (Hewing 2020, Lorenzen 2016), but the coverage guarantee in this construction does **not** come from propagating a faithful sensor-noise covariance through the dynamics. The diagnostic in ADR 0009 shows σ²_published is the framework's √-compressed Kalman+inflation state, not a faithful covariance of the measurement noise. The coverage guarantee instead comes from fitting δ_cal on a held-out calibration corpus to match the empirical lower-tail mass to Φ(−λ) — a **split-conformal calibration of the additive offset**, applied on top of the chance-constraint-shaped margin. The construction is therefore a hybrid: chance-constraint *functional form* + split-conformal *coverage guarantee* with the exchangeability assumption shifted onto the calibration corpus rather than carried by a propagated covariance.

This is distinct from the CVaR-in-cost mechanism of Fan et al. 2021 (STEP), which keeps the position-risk constraint relaxable and routes risk awareness through the optimiser's objective; here the constraint stays hard and the (calibrated) variance enters as a deterministic margin. It is also distinct from pure split-conformal prediction (which would produce a non-parametric quantile margin without using the published σ²) — the variance-aware shape gives the margin spatial structure (tighter where the published σ² is small), and conformal calibration only fits the additive offset that the σ² shape misses on average.

The chance-constraint formula `ε = ε_base + δ_cal + λ √σ²_c` survives the diagnostic; the **interpretation** of `λ √σ²_c` shifts. It is no longer "λ standard deviations of LiDAR measurement noise" — it is "λ standard deviations of the framework's published Kalman+inflation state, which encodes a √-compressed view of the underlying noise plus a staleness term, modulated by sensing-geometry factors." Coverage interpretability at the chance-constraint level is restored by δ_cal under exchangeability of the calibration corpus and deployment data; sensitivity to α_d alone is not, and the construction does not claim it. Downstream consumers of `f(x, y, s)` that reason about α_d-driven uncertainty (e.g., a runtime LiDAR-quality monitor) need to do so through the conditional-on-cadence residual model, not through the published σ².

The behaviour of ε with map state follows from the variance term, with one cleanly-stated direction: **σ²_published tracks sensing confidence, not feature complexity**. ADR 0009 establishes that the framework's per-point noise model `α_d · rz²` is distance-only — it does not see local ceiling curvature, beam-edge transitions, or within-cell planar deviation. The variance therefore grows where the *sensing* is poor and shrinks where it is good:

- Tighter ε (high σ²_c, larger margin) at FOV edges and long-range cells (large rz inflates σ²_meas), at stale cells (`Δt` large, time-inflation contribution dominates), at high-outlier-rate cells (the Mahalanobis-rejected branch's σ² floor lifts), and at near-grazing incidence (oblique rays mean longer rz to the surface).
- Relaxed ε (low σ²_c) over densely-observed, near-normally-sensed surfaces — including over the near face of a beam, which is **closer** to the sensor than the surrounding flat ceiling and therefore has lower σ²_meas. The feature geometry there is handled by `c` itself dropping below `h_base + s` (the beam's lower z-bottom enters the elevation channel via the suppression rule); ε is not what catches it.

The within-cell geometric spread that would matter physically at beam edges or slab seams is, in this construction, routed into δ_cal as a non-spatial constant offset. That means flat interiors carry the same extra margin as edges — a defensible conservatism (it never under-covers) but explicit: ε does not become spatially selective on geometric complexity. If a future iteration needs that selectivity, a roughness-per-cell or normal-residual layer would supply it, at the cost of a new perception channel and the "no additional perception cost" principle the construction currently honours.

The constraint boundary tracks the **map's confidence**, not the worst case envisaged at design time. That is the substantive improvement over the static ε of reviewed work; it is not equivalent to a complexity-tracking margin and the difference matters when reading downstream behaviour.

## Design choices

### Controller-agnostic interface

The downstream-facing object of this stage is `f(x, y, s)` and its gradient, not a per-controller specialisation. The package boundary follows: the runtime node publishes `f` as a `grid_map_msgs/GridMap` layer plus an auxiliary clearance message, and exposes a CasADi B-spline interpolant computed from the same field. Either the lead NMPC or any HMPC drop-in consumes through the same interface. This is the architectural commitment that keeps the controller-family decision live (see [controller-family wording](../journal/2026-W21.md) and 03 for the policy).

### Fused single-pass CUDA kernel for the field computation

The field computation is a sequence of cell-wise operations: subtract the two elevation layers to get `c`, sum the two variance layers to get `σ²_c`, square-root and scale to get the variance contribution to ε, add the constants `ε_base + δ_cal`, subtract `c − h_base − s − ε` to get `f`. Each is O(cells), each reads the same two pairs of layers, and the only loop-carried dependency is the running computation of ε before subtracting it into `f`. A fused single-pass kernel runs the whole sequence per cell in registers, with the only global-memory accesses being the four input reads and the two output writes (`f` and an auxiliary `clearance` layer). The lit-review allocation in Table 6 is 0.1 ms per update at 10 Hz; the budget is realistic for a fused kernel on the Orin Nano Super's 200×200 grid (the Kalman update kernels in 01 are bandwidth-bound at similar grid sizes and dominate the per-cycle cost by two orders of magnitude). A separated-pass design — four small kernels, one per arithmetic stage — pays four launch-overhead costs and four global-memory round-trips for no benefit; the fused design is the right default.

The kernel is parameterised on `s` so a sweep over the discrete s-profile values that 03's warm-start emits (3–5 values across the prismatic range) costs as many kernel launches as values, against the same input layers. This is the same kernel reused; no separate construction.

### CasADi B-spline interpolant: cubic, regular knot spacing

The controller queries `f` at arbitrary `(x, y, s)` along the prediction horizon, typically at finer spatial resolution than the elevation grid. The field must be C¹ at minimum (the NMPC requirement; see lit-review §7.4) and gradient-queryable analytically rather than by finite difference (the QP backend needs Jacobians, not finite-difference approximations whose error term enters the constraint linearisation). A cubic tensor-product B-spline over the grid satisfies both: it is C² globally, its first and second partials are also cubic B-splines and exact under CasADi's symbolic differentiation, and CasADi's `interpolant` factory exports the spline as a `CasADi::Function` consumable by the acados problem builder.

The form is committed: tensor-product cubic B-spline, regular knot grid co-located with the elevation grid, periodic boundary conditions disabled (the map is finite). The free parameter — knot spacing relative to the elevation grid resolution — is not committed up front. A 1:1 spacing (one knot per elevation cell) is the natural starting point and matches the spatial density of the underlying observations; coarser knot spacing reduces spline evaluation cost in the controller at the cost of smoothing real features. Pick during phase-3 profiling against the controller solve time.

### Calibration protocol for δ_cal

The lit review (§11.6 phase 2) names δ_cal as fitted offline against held-out indoor ceiling sequences but does not specify the corpus, the ground-truth source, or the statistical objective. The protocol proposed here is:

*Corpus.* Three surface classes covered at the industrial partner facility (the same site used in phase 6 hardware validation): textured concrete (the dominant indoor case), texture-poor finishes (plasterboard, paint), and oblique-incidence sequences with mean ray angle exceeding 60° from surface normal. Ten to twenty minutes of recording per class, with the platform driving a varied trajectory beneath instrumented ceiling reference points. The recording set is held out from any sequence used elsewhere in the pipeline.

*Corpus extension — dense vs sparse observation.* If the sim-phase joint sweep of validation check #3 (suppression-rule firing rate) in 01 returns a low firing rate under the operating cell size, the calibration corpus must additionally split each surface class into densely- and sparsely-observed sequences of the same patch. The densely-observed pass drives slowly past the ceiling reference points with sustained dwell, maximising points-per-cell-per-scan; the sparse pass traverses at normal operating speed. The residual distributions of the two passes characterise the mean-vs-min bias term in δ_cal: if the dense pass residuals show only sensor-model under-reporting and the sparse pass residuals show that plus a directional offset, the offset is the bias contribution. Estimating it directly from the corpus is preferable to inferring it analytically from ceiling-curvature priors. The split is conditional — if the sim check shows suppression fires reliably at the operating resolution, the original three-class corpus is sufficient and the dense/sparse split is omitted. This contingency is pre-stated here so the calibration design does not need to be redone after the sim measurement lands.

*Ground truth.* Total-station measurement of each ceiling reference point against the platform's `odom` frame, using the same total-station rig planned for phase 6. For each reference point and each frame in which the corresponding ceiling cell is observed, the measured `z_ref` is the ground truth; the Kalman estimate `ẑ_ceil` and its variance `σ²_zceil` come from the published ceiling map.

*Scope of the calibration — ceiling-only as a working assumption.* The margin uses the composed `√σ²_c = √(σ²_zceil + σ²_zfloor)`, but the protocol below fits δ_cal on ceiling-only residuals against σ²_zceil. The implicit assumption is `σ²_zfloor ≪ σ²_zceil` so that `√σ²_c ≈ √σ²_zceil` and the ceiling-only calibration approximately covers the composed margin. The assumption is defensible at HILDA's geometry — floor returns are short-range, near-normally-incident, and dense, so the floor's √-floor sits well below the ceiling's — but it is unverified on the partner-facility surfaces. The corpus collection adds a verification pass: record `σ²_zfloor` per evaluation cell alongside the ceiling residuals; if the floor's published σ² is empirically within ~30 % of the ceiling's on any surface class, repeat the calibration protocol independently on the floor layer (analogous corpus, floor ground-truth via total-station shots of the trafficked surface, separate `δ_cal_floor`). The composed margin then becomes `λ √(σ²_zceil + σ²_zfloor) + δ_cal_ceil + δ_cal_floor`. The ceiling-only path is preferred if the assumption holds; the protocol is *prepared* to extend if it does not.

*Freshness-conditional binning (per ADR 0009).* The published σ² carries an inflation contribution proportional to time-since-last-observation, so a δ_cal fit against pooled residuals would under-cover stale cells and over-cover fresh ones. Before the fit, partition the corpus residuals by per-cell time-since-last-observation `Δt` into a small number of bins (proposed: ≤ 0.5 s, 0.5–2 s, 2–5 s, > 5 s — the boundaries chosen so each bin holds at least 10 % of the residual mass and the bins span the deployment-realistic cadence range). The published per-cell `update_time` layer carries the timestamp of each cell's last update; `Δt` per residual is the difference between the metric-evaluation time and that timestamp. The fit then produces either (a) per-bin `δ_cal[k]` with the runtime looking up the bin from the live `update_time` layer, or (b) a single δ_cal fit at the binning mode (typically Δt ∈ 0.5–2 s, the densely-observed regime) with a documented coverage degradation in the stale bins. Choice between (a) and (b) is corpus-driven — if the per-bin δ_cal differ by more than ~ε_base in fitted value, the lookup is worth the runtime complexity; if they agree within ε_base, the single constant is sufficient. Decide after the corpus is in hand.

*Operating-point lock.* The fitted δ_cal is conditional on the deployment values of `time_variance` and `update_variance_fps` (ADR 0009 §F2). Both are recorded in the calibration manifest. On the runtime side, a startup-time audit compares the calibration manifest's values against the live ceiling-config values and refuses to publish `f` until they match; this prevents silently consuming a calibration that no longer applies. The same applies to any future change to `sensor_noise_factor` — although `sensor_noise_factor` enters only as √α_d through the floor, the calibration's coverage guarantees collapse if the deployment value differs from calibration.

*Objective.* δ_cal is the offset (per-bin or constant per the freshness-conditional choice above; optionally also per-surface-class if the residual distributions differ materially) that makes the empirical **lower-tail** residual mass consistent with the chance constraint's one-sided coverage at λ = 3. The dangerous case is `ẑ_ceil > z_ref` — estimated ceiling above the true ceiling means less clearance than the planner thinks. Define the residual `r = z_ref − ẑ_ceil`; the dangerous tail is `r < −(λ√σ²_zceil + δ_cal)`. Within each (Δt, surface) bin, find δ_cal that minimises

$$
\bigl| \,P\!\left(\,r < -\bigl(\lambda\sqrt{\sigma^2_{z_{\text{ceil}}}} + \delta_{\text{cal}}\bigr)\right) \;-\; \Phi(-\lambda) \,\bigr|
$$

on the held-out set. The objective is one-sided (matches the chance constraint's directional semantics) and coverage-based rather than second-moment-based (makes no Gaussian assumption beyond what the Kalman update implicitly enforces). Earlier drafts wrote this as a two-sided `|·|` exceedance — that targets twice the dangerous-tail mass and fits a δ_cal roughly twice as large as needed; corrected here per the same coverage-vs-empirical-rule check that fixed the 99.7 % → 99.865 % quotation in §Theory.

*Validation.* Two ablations in phase 4 of the research plan: (i) `δ_cal = 0, λ = 0` tests whether the fitted δ_cal closes a real coverage gap; (ii) `δ_cal` fitted at one cadence bin but deployed across all bins tests whether the freshness-conditional split is load-bearing or whether the single-constant alternative suffices.

This protocol exists in this document and not in the lit review; it is a substantive deliverable for the thesis-stage methodology beyond what §11.2 carries. ADR 0009 added the freshness-conditional step and the operating-point lock on the basis of the 01c diagnostic.

### Soft constraint at the field level, slack at the controller

The field publishes `f(x, y, s)` without a sign-flip; the controller (03) consumes `f ≥ 0` as a soft path constraint with an L1 slack penalty in the OCP. This keeps the field semantics clean — `f` is the feasibility margin, positive everywhere safe, negative where the platform would collide — and isolates the soft-vs-hard policy at the boundary where it can be tuned against solver feasibility under transient perception artefacts. A separate "barrier" channel (the relaxed log-barrier term in 03's cost) is computed in the controller from the same `f`, not exported by this stage; centralising the constraint shape in the controller keeps the perception side controller-agnostic.

## Interface

**Inputs.**

- `/elevation_map/floor` — `grid_map_msgs/GridMap`, from 01 at the upstream elevation-mapping rate (~10 Hz on the verified 3-node bag-replay path), with `elevation` and `variance` layers.
- `/elevation_map/ceiling` — `grid_map_msgs/GridMap`, from 01 at the same rate, with `ceiling_height` (sign-restored) and `variance` layers.
- Parameters: `h_base`, `eps_base`, `delta_cal` (or `delta_cal[surface_class]` if per-class), `lambda` (default 3), spline knot spacing.

**Outputs.**

- `/constraint_field` — `grid_map_msgs/GridMap` at the post-sync rate (~7.5–8.3 Hz measured on the verified bag-replay path; ApproximateTime synchronizer loss accounts for the gap from the 10 Hz upstream — see §Implementation status *Live-pipeline rate verification*) with `clearance`, `variance` (composed σ²_c), `epsilon`, `feasibility` (= f) layers, plus the same set evaluated at a small set of representative `s` values for diagnostic visualisation.
- `/ceiling_clearance` — `hilda_msgs/CeilingClearance` at the same rate: a compact per-cycle summary (minimum clearance in the local map, position of the minimum, indicator of any cells where f < 0 at the current `s`). Consumed by `ceiling_collision_monitor` and the HMI.
- CasADi B-spline export — produced in-process from the latest `f` field, consumed by the controller process either through a shared file handle, an in-process Python import, or a serialised parameter blob over DDS. Mechanism decided in 05 against the cross-distro DDS profile.

**Frames and rates.** All grids in `odom`; outputs share the cell layout of the input elevation grids (no resampling). Publication tracks the upstream GridMap publish rate — `map_acquire_fps = 5.0` on both elevation instances — minus the ApproximateTime synchroniser's matching loss: **~5 Hz at `/constraint_field`**, measured 2026-05-29 on the bag-replay path with zero synchroniser rejections. (An earlier "~8 Hz against 10 Hz upstream" figure conflated `update_pose_fps = 10.0` with the GridMap publish rate `map_acquire_fps = 5.0`; corrected here.) The spline update is gated by the same cadence.

**CeilingClearance-at-robot-pose follow-up landed.** *2026-05-27.* The `clearance_pub_` topic `/ceiling_clearance` is now populated from a TF lookup at every syncCallback. Spec: [docs/superpowers/specs/2026-05-27-ceiling-clearance-at-robot-pose-design.md](superpowers/specs/2026-05-27-ceiling-clearance-at-robot-pose-design.md). Key design choices:

- *f recomputed node-side, not read from the kernel's `feasibility` layer.* The layer embeds the kernel-side `h_base` and `s_ext` at launch time; reading it would couple the message's `is_safe` to scalar values that may diverge from the node's current `h_base_` and `s_` (stale launch, default mismatch, future `/reference_controller/state` wiring landing asymmetrically). Sampling only `clearance` and `epsilon` from the map, then computing `f = c − h_base − s − ε` node-side, keeps each scalar sourced exactly once.
- *NaN-encoded infeasibility for `max_feasible_height`.* When `c − h_base − ε ≤ 0` no feasible sledge height exists; the message reports `NaN`, not `0.0`. Clamp-at-0 collides "barely feasible, extend to ~0" with "no feasible height exists" and picks the safe-looking value for the infeasible case — a silent false-safe. NaN matches the stack's NaN-propagation convention, separates the two meanings, and fails in the safe direction under comparison.
- *Sledge-travel cap.* `max_feasible_height = min(c − h_base − ε, s_phys_max)` with `s_phys_max = 1.28 m` from the URDF's `linear_joint` upper limit. "Max feasible height" implies a *deployable* height; reporting beyond mechanical travel misleads in the opposite direction.
- *Fail-loud skip with severity split.* TF lookup failure and out-of-map are WARN (2 s throttle; genuine faults). NaN cell directly overhead is INFO (10 s throttle; expected steady state during exploration and in open areas). All three skip the `/ceiling_clearance` publish; `/constraint_field` continues so the consumer can tell node-alive-but-sample-failed from node-dead. See memory [[feedback-log-severity-by-failure-class]] and [[feedback-latching-spatial-samples]].
- *Infeasibility-at-pose is published, not skipped.* A cell where `f ≤ 0` is exactly what `/ceiling_clearance` exists to signal; skipping that cycle would leave the consumer's cached message at the previous (safe) state. So infeasibility publishes with `is_safe=false`, `feasibility=0.0`, `max_feasible_height=NaN`.

Structural ratio `/ceiling_clearance : /constraint_field = 1.00` (no skip events fired). **Ceiling-layer sign bug + h_base datum corrected.** *2026-05-29.* A systematic review of the Phase-A runtime found two coupled defects the sign-off did not cover, both now fixed — see [decisions/0011-ceiling-layer-selection-and-hbase-datum.md](decisions/0011-ceiling-layer-selection-and-hbase-datum.md).
- *Wrong ceiling layer.* `syncCallback` read a single `elevation_layer` (`elevation`) from *both* maps, but the ceiling instance's `elevation` is the splitter's negated raw value; the decoded positive layer `ceiling_height` (published by `ceiling_decode` explicitly "for downstream consumers (Phase 2 constraint field)") was never read. The node computed `clearance = (−z_ceil) − z_floor` — sign-inverted for every cell. Fix: a per-map `ceiling_elevation_layer` parameter (default `ceiling_height`), presence-checked per map; the floor's `elevation_layer` is unchanged.
- *h_base datum.* The runtime config carried `h_base = 0.85 m`, physically impossible as a mast-top height (the driving column alone is 1.64 m tall; the drill-tip offset is 1.889 m, `calibration_drilly_v2.yaml`). Per §Notation `h_base ≈ 1.99 m` (mast-top above floor at s=0). The placeholder under-counted the platform by ≈1.14 m, biasing `f` optimistic — the dangerous direction. Corrected to 1.99 m (config + node default); the precise FK value is flagged for confirmation against `kinematic_model.md` §5.2.

Why the sign-off missed it: the signed-off 02a/02b experiments run through the Python `compute_ceiling_metrics.py` path (which decodes the ceiling correctly via `h_ceil = −elevation`), and the C++ node had no integration test exercising the assembled callback. The fix adds one — `syncCallback`'s map-processing core was extracted into `buildConstraintField(floor, ceiling, out)`, and `test_build_constraint_field.cpp` feeds a floor+ceiling GridMap pair (ceiling `elevation` negated, `ceiling_height` positive) through it and asserts the output clearance equals the true geometric clearance. The test was written first and reproduced the bug (clearance −2.4 m) before the fix; all package tests now pass (6 kernel + 5 publish + 2 integration = 13/13).

Phase A "sim-phase signed off" is amended: the kernel ε-math stood, but geometric clearance through the node was wrong. **Re-verified 2026-05-29** by replaying ~80 s of the persistent bag through the node (splitter → dual elevation mapping → `constraint_field_node`, `use_sim_time`, split thresholds 2.05/2.35) and capturing `/ceiling_clearance` — the C++ node path, not the Python metrics path: 133 samples, clearance ∈ [3.094, 3.222] m, **zero negative**; feasibility split 91 safe / 42 infeasible (the latter early high-variance cells, correctly tightened); zero geometry-mismatch or skip faults. The field is geometrically correct through the node.

Rate caveat surfaced by the same run: `/constraint_field` clocked **4.95 Hz, not the ~8 Hz** stated above and in §Frames and rates. Both elevation instances publish at `map_acquire_fps: 5.0`, so two 5 Hz streams through the ApproximateTime synchroniser give ~5 Hz — the measured value, with zero synchroniser rejections. The earlier "~8 Hz at `/constraint_field` against 10 Hz upstream" figures conflate `update_pose_fps` (10.0) with the GridMap publish rate `map_acquire_fps` (5.0). The thesis-citable `/constraint_field` rate at the current config is **~5 Hz**; raising `map_acquire_fps` to 10.0 on both instances would recover ~8 Hz if the controller-side consumer needs it. Reconciliation (correct the doc to ~5 Hz, or raise the fps) is a pending call, flagged not silently rewritten.

**CasADi B-spline export landed 2026-05-30** (ADR-0010 deliverable #1; `hilda_clearance_field/clearance_spline.py`, branch `feature/clearance-spline-export`). `build_clearance_spline(snapshot, params)` fits two bivariate cubic B-splines over the published `clearance` and `epsilon` layers and emits a `casadi.Function` `f(x, y, s[, h]) = c − ε − H` (`H = h + z_tip0 + s`, exactly affine in `s`/`h`, so `∂f/∂s = ∂f/∂h = −1`) plus an analytic gradient, conservative-infeasible outside the map (pad-ring + `if_else`), with NaN→infeasible-at-fit. The interface shape, the `h_base`→`H` rename (`z_tip0 = 1.899 m` FK-exact, `h₀` an operating-point value, and the §Notation-vs-§3 `h₀` tension flagged for reconciliation), and the conservative-edge/NaN policy are recorded in [the design spec](superpowers/specs/2026-05-30-clearance-spline-export-design.md) and [decisions/0013-clearance-spline-interface.md](decisions/0013-clearance-spline-interface.md). Verified by TDD parity against the prototype kernel (cell-centre `f` to ≤1e-6) and an acados SQP-RTI consumability gate (`f` as `con_h_expr` → status 0); 20 export tests pass alongside the 13 kernel tests. `λ` is baked into the interpolated `ε` (the live-`λ` variant — interpolate σ²_c — is documented, not built). δ_cal calibration scaffolding and the `grid_map_msgs → GridSnapshot` adapter remain deferred — the adapter to the module-03/05 integration, where it is verified against a real `/constraint_field` bag; online coefficient refresh and cross-process transport are 05 questions.

## Open questions

### Sim-phase

- ~~**Package boundary.**~~ *Closed 2026-05-26* by [decisions/0010-clearance-field-package-boundary.md](decisions/0010-clearance-field-package-boundary.md). Split by role: runtime publishing node stays in `hilda_ceiling/ceiling_constraint_field/`; `hilda_clearance_field/` owns the CasADi B-spline export, δ_cal calibration scaffolding, and Python/CuPy prototype kernel. See §Implementation status for the boundary specification.
- **Spline knot spacing.** Committed form (cubic tensor-product, regular knots); free parameter (knot density relative to grid). Pick during phase-3 profiling against the controller solve time.
- **Controller-side B-spline transport.** Whether the spline reaches the UDOO via shared file, in-process import, or DDS parameter blob is a 05 question; the choice affects the field-update-to-spline-availability latency.
- **Provisional λ and ε_base for sim work.** Until δ_cal is fitted, the chance-constraint identity is parameterised at λ = 3 and δ_cal = 0. This is structurally fine for sim — the construction's behaviour as a function of variance is what the sim tests, not its absolute coverage on the partner facility's surfaces — but every controller experiment that consumes `f` from this stage in sim must explicitly carry the "δ_cal pending hardware calibration" annotation in its results manifest. Not a research open question; a discipline-of-results question.
- ~~**Calibration pre-floor (α_d sensitivity of σ²_published).**~~ *Closed 2026-05-26.* Settled by `sim_validation_01c` and diagnosed at the code level in [decisions/0009-variance-pipeline-alpha-d-diagnostic.md](decisions/0009-variance-pipeline-alpha-d-diagnostic.md). σ²_published is the framework's √-compressed Kalman+inflation state, not the LiDAR noise variance; δ_cal scope revised accordingly above. Corroborating experiment `sim_validation_01d/time_variance_sweep` queued to confirm the √-mechanism empirically before the calibration corpus is collected.
- **Per-cadence δ_cal vs single constant.** Pending the partner-facility corpus. The freshness-conditional binning step is committed in §Calibration protocol; whether the per-bin offsets actually differ enough to justify the runtime lookup (vs a single constant fit at the binning mode with documented coverage degradation in the stale bins) is a corpus-driven decision. Hard-bound to the hardware sprint.
- **Dual-instance origin drift — deferred grid resample.** The wire-in's one-cell origin-alignment tolerance absorbs the sub-cell drift between the two `elevation_mapping_cupy` instances on the sim bag, but the principled fix is to resample one map onto the other's grid before composition rather than tolerating the smear. Production-deployment item, not a sim-phase blocker: the smear is well below ε's per-cell scale and the structural-behaviour experiments 02a/02b consume the field unaffected. Lift into a follow-up commit once the controller-side consumer is wired in and the smear's downstream effect on the spline gradient can be measured rather than estimated.
- **Suppression-firing rate on the ceiling instance.** *Partially closed by 01b.* The joint sweep at cs=0.15 m showed the rule contributes ~6 mm to RMSE_boundary but is flat in threshold across {5, 10, 20, 50} — the threshold within this range is not the lever that tunes the contribution. ADR 0009 sharpens the consequence: the within-cell geometric spread that the mean-vs-min bias was meant to absorb is now unconditional (it enters δ_cal regardless of whether the rule fires), so the dense/sparse corpus split is load-bearing rather than contingent. Sim-side measurement done; calibration-side consequence committed in §Calibration protocol's *Corpus extension*.

### Hardware-deferred

These are calibration-bound: the empirical signal they want comes from real sensor noise interacting with real localisation drift on the partner facility's surfaces. Sim-data substitutes would measure only what Gazebo's noise model + ground-truth poses produce — a self-consistency check, not a validation.

- **Independence of floor-ceiling observation noise at the Kalman-channel level.** The additive variance composition `σ²_c = σ²_zceil + σ²_zfloor` assumes it; the case for independence is stronger at the Kalman channel (separate returns, separate count thresholds, separate incidence-angle models) than for the full per-cell position covariance (which is shared via the robot pose). The right empirical test is a correlation check on rosbag pairs of variance maps under stationary observation on hardware; if material covariance shows up at the Kalman level, σ²_c gets a covariance term and the calibration refits. Defer to the hardware sprint.
- **δ_cal calibration corpus.** Three surface classes at the partner facility (textured concrete, texture-poor finishes, oblique-incidence sequences), total-station ground truth, coverage-based objective at λ = 3 (protocol fully specified above). All four inputs are hardware-bound. Defer to the hardware sprint.
- **Per-surface-class δ_cal vs single constant.** The choice between a single global δ_cal and a per-surface-class lookup is answered by the calibration corpus, not by design. Decide when the corpus is in hand.
- **Empirical chance-constraint coverage.** λ = 3 corresponds to 99.7% nominal coverage under the Kalman-Gaussian assumption. The fitted δ_cal recovers nominal coverage on the calibration corpus by construction, but coverage on out-of-distribution surfaces (specular finishes, glass) is unverified. Sanity run on phase-6 hardware-validation data.

Everything in this hardware-deferred block is real research work, but none of it gates sim-phase progress. The construction has been designed to consume any δ_cal value the calibration eventually produces; the sim experiments stress the *structural* behaviour (does ε(x, y) propagate correctly to the controller? does the C¹ B-spline interface return sensible gradients? does the OCP solve under varying ε? does the controller behave sensibly when σ²_c spikes locally?) without committing specific calibration values.
