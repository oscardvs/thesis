**Worked.** All three PDFs fetched and read in full.

- *Buchanan 2019* — arXiv PDF over HTTPS, 8 pages. §III-B is unambiguous: one Grid Map data structure with two per-cell layers `[ĥ_f, σ²_f]` and `[ĥ_c, σ²_c]`, per-point Bayesian classification (Gaussian likelihood × body-height-conditioned prior `P(E)`) routes each return into the corresponding layer's Kalman filter (eqn 5). The contrast with this thesis's z-inversion-into-two-framework-instances is *mechanistic* (partition returns by classification vs partition insertions by sign), not topological. The "no published work uses z-inversion for ceiling mapping" framing survives; the language in 01 needed to be sharpened to name the partition mechanism explicitly instead of describing Buchanan as "separate `grid_map` instances" (he isn't).

- *Miki 2022* — arXiv PDF (25 MB; direct curl worked where WebFetch had failed yesterday). §II-C is more nuanced than the lit-review "maximum-height kernel" shorthand: the framework runs a one-dimensional Kalman fusion (eqn 1) with a count-thresholded suppression rule that drops lower returns when many points fall in the same cell within one scan. Behaviour approaches max-height under dense sampling but is not literally a max kernel. The variance update (eqn 2) is the standard recursive Kalman variance plus a constant time-variance increment `σ²_t` per cycle for un-updated cells, which is the operational mechanism by which stale cells return to the outlier-rejection envelope. Independently corroborates ADR 0005: visibility cleanup (eqn 3, `if pzi < hi − σi, remove`) is *designed* to remove cells below the ray-height envelope, so applied to negated-z ceiling returns it would systematically erase the lowest overhead surface. Worth noting that Miki's framework explicitly treats overhead structure as nuisance (§II-C "exclusion area", Fig. 3) — the framework is not just silent on ceilings, it is actively designed to suppress them, which makes the contribution claim of the dual-instance reuse stronger.

- *Fankhauser 2018* — fetched via Wayback Machine snapshot of the ETH Research Collection bitstream URL after direct fetch returned the SPA HTML stub (recipe in surfaced section). 9 pages, full primary source. The variance decomposition is exactly what 02 assumed: measurement variance `σ_p² = J_S Σ_S J_S^T + J_Φ Σ_ΦIS J_Φ^T` (eqn 5; sensor-noise + sensor-rotation contributions, sensor-position uncertainty deliberately excluded by the robot-centric frame choice), Kalman update `σ_h^{2+} = σ_h^{2-} σ_p² / (σ_h^{2-} + σ_p²)` (eqn 6), and per-cell spatial covariance Σ_P_i accumulated through robot motion in eqns 14, 20–21. Critically: the full drift-aware fusion (h_min, h_max in §III-D) is a *map-fusion-step* construction, *not* exposed by the framework's runtime `variance` layer. The runtime layer publishes the per-cell Kalman variance only. This is the load-bearing distinction for what 02's δ_cal is calibrated to absorb.

**Surfaced.**

- **Reframing of what δ_cal absorbs.** Pre-correction 02 described δ_cal as absorbing "documented under-reporting of the Kalman variance on texture-poor and oblique surfaces" — true, but partial. The drift-aware Σ_P_i lives in the map-fusion step Fankhauser §III-D; `elevation_mapping_cupy` does not expose it; the published per-cell variance is therefore measurement-noise-driven only. δ_cal is doing two jobs: (i) absorbing measurement-model mis-specification (the original claim), and (ii) absorbing the absence of localisation drift from the runtime variance interface. Both have to be calibrated against ground-truth residuals on the partner facility's instrumented sequences; the protocol Riccardo signed off on yesterday still works, but the interpretation of what is being calibrated is now precise. 02 updated accordingly.

- **Variance-independence reframing.** 01 and 02 both previously asserted "floor and ceiling observation noise are independent — separate sensor returns, different incidence angles, no shared structural error." The Fankhauser decomposition makes a sharper claim possible: independence is defensible at the *Kalman-channel* level (separate returns, separate count thresholds, separate incidence-angle models) but *not* on the unmodelled drift channel (both layers share the robot pose; drift is shared). The additive variance composition `σ²_c = σ²_zceil + σ²_zfloor` is correct for what the framework publishes, and δ_cal absorbs the shared-drift component on average. Updated in both 01 (open questions reframed at Kalman-channel level) and 02 (open questions reframed to make this distinction).

- **Operational recipe — paywalled PDFs behind SPA frontends.** ETH Research Collection migrated to a DSpace 7 / Angular SPA between 2024 and now; all `/bitstream/...pdf` URLs return 782 bytes of HTML 200 regardless of headers. Semantic Scholar's openAccessPdf field reports the SPA URL as canonical. The reliable recipe is the Wayback Machine: `http://archive.org/wayback/available?url=<encoded URL>` → follow `archived_snapshots.closest.url` → curl with `if_/` suffix between the timestamp and the original URL. The `if_/` modifier returns the raw archived response, bypassing Wayback's wrapper. Worked for Fankhauser 2018; recipe worth keeping for any future ETH or DSpace-mirror PDF that the live site no longer serves.
