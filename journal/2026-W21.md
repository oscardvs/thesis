- *Miki 2022* — arXiv PDF (25 MB; direct curl worked where WebFetch had failed yesterday). §II-C is more nuanced than the lit-review "maximum-height kernel" shorthand: the framework runs a one-dimensional Kalman fusion (eqn 1) with a count-thresholded suppression rule that drops lower returns when many points fall in the same cell within one scan. Behaviour approaches max-height under dense sampling but is not literally a max kernel. The variance update (eqn 2) is the standard recursive Kalman variance plus a constant time-variance increment `σ²_t` per cycle for un-updated cells, which is the operational mechanism by which stale cells return to the outlier-rejection envelope. Independently corroborates ADR 0005: visibility cleanup (eqn 3, `if pzi < hi − σi, remove`) is *designed* to remove cells below the ray-height envelope, so applied to negated-z ceiling returns it would systematically erase the lowest overhead surface. Worth noting that Miki's framework explicitly treats overhead structure as nuisance (§II-C "exclusion area", Fig. 3) — the framework is not just silent on ceilings, it is actively designed to suppress them, which makes the contribution claim of the dual-instance reuse stronger.

- *Fankhauser 2018* — fetched via Wayback Machine snapshot of the ETH Research Collection bitstream URL after direct fetch returned the SPA HTML stub (recipe in surfaced section). 9 pages, full primary source. The variance decomposition is exactly what 02 assumed: measurement variance `σ_p² = J_S Σ_S J_S^T + J_Φ Σ_ΦIS J_Φ^T` (eqn 5; sensor-noise + sensor-rotation contributions, sensor-position uncertainty deliberately excluded by the robot-centric frame choice), Kalman update `σ_h^{2+} = σ_h^{2-} σ_p² / (σ_h^{2-} + σ_p²)` (eqn 6), and per-cell spatial covariance Σ_P_i accumulated through robot motion in eqns 14, 20–21. Critically: the full drift-aware fusion (h_min, h_max in §III-D) is a *map-fusion-step* construction, *not* exposed by the framework's runtime `variance` layer. The runtime layer publishes the per-cell Kalman variance only. This is the load-bearing distinction for what 02's δ_cal is calibrated to absorb.

F5's "framework-side, deferred" diagnosis from the audit can now be retired: the −0.18 m bias was a manifestation of F1's frame mismatch (multi-surface averaging from the [2.05, 2.35] dead-band landing on the wrong cells in base_link-frame space) and/or the off-production `wall_num_thresh=100`. The framework-side investigation flagged in F5 is no longer load-bearing.

*2. `RMSE_core` drops dramatically.* At production cell size (0.10 m, edge_sharpen=true): **0.200 m** corrected vs **0.300 m** misconfigured. A 100 mm / 33 % reduction. At 0.30 m cells the corrected `RMSE_core` is 0.147 m (better still). The hardware-grade acceptance threshold from `joint_sweep.yaml`'s `acceptance_thresholds.rmse_core_max_m: 0.15` is now within reach at the coarser cell sizes; at production 0.10 m we are at 0.20 m, off by 33 %. The remaining gap is plausibly absorbable by δ_cal at the calibration step per 02's protocol, but it is no longer dominated by configuration error.

*3. The suppression rule does contribute at production resolution.* On/off comparison of `RMSE_boundary` at each cell size, corrected sweep:

| cell | RMSE_b(T) | RMSE_b(F) | Δ(T−F) |
|------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| 0.10 | 0.6566 | 0.6609 | −4.3 mm |
| 0.15 | 0.6046 | 0.6109 | −6.3 mm |
| 0.20 | 0.5693 | 0.5753 | −6.0 mm |
| 0.30 | 0.5444 | 0.5586 | **−14.2 mm** |

All deltas negative — `enable_edge_sharpen=true` strictly improves boundary RMSE at every tested cell size. The README's falsifiable prediction (`RMSE_boundary[T] < RMSE_boundary[F]` at production cell size if suppression fires) is empirically supported. The contribution at 0.10 m is small (4.3 mm) — consistent with the RoboSense Airy 96 density math saying the rule rarely fires at 0.10 m × `wall_num_thresh=20`. The contribution grows ~3× at 0.30 m where points-per-cell-per-scan rises into the rule's regime. The "rule firing" architectural claim survives; the *magnitude* at production resolution is small enough that the `wall_num_thresh` extension sweep (`sim_validation_01b/`) is the right next experiment to find the operating point that maximises the contribution.

A trade-off surfaces at coarser cells. `RMSE_core` deltas (T − F): −7 mm at 0.10, −10 mm at 0.15, −10 mm at 0.20, **+18 mm at 0.30**. At 0.30 m cells the suppression rule fires aggressively enough that it drops some valid interior returns and adds noise on flat cells — a net loss on core RMSE despite the boundary win. So the sweet spot is between 0.15 and 0.20 m cells where boundary improves by ~6 mm AND core improves by ~10 mm. Worth surfacing as an operating-point recommendation when the `wall_num_thresh` sweep lands.
